Take That shows support for A Siblings Wish!

It is that time of the year when big and accomplished singers and bands come together at Hyde Park to entertain their fans and interact with them. In short, to have a gala time! This year, British Summertime Hyde Park festival saw some amazing performances from Nathan Sykes, Mumford & Sons, and Jamie Lawson.

But festivals are not merely for performances and gigs, sometimes, they also give us moments worth cherishing. People witnessed one such moment at Hyde Park from none other than the iconic band, Take That.

Here at CelebMix, we have been posting and informing readers about A Siblings Wish. The Harvey Hext Trust – A Siblings Wish is

“A non-profit charity raising funds to supply individual personalised Memory boxes to bereaved children.”

A Siblings Wish acknowledges the void that occurs after a sibling passes away. It offers siblings a chance to keep all the happy memories in one place that is, in a ‘wooden box’ designed and offered by the trust. The trust is in the memory of Harvey Hext who died of aggressive Sarcoma on 24th November 2015. The trust has support from a lot of people and organizations.

Today, it got one more supporter. A Siblings Wish was at Hyde Park to attend the music festival and got a chance to meet Take That. Later, the trust shared some pictures on its Facebook page.

The band stole the show not only through its performance but also through this wonderful deed.
